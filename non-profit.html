A redesign of the nonprofit organizations website One Small Step/Clothes Cabin, focusing on clearer branding, navigation, and legitimacy.

12/14/21-12/17/21 Present

The current Clothes Cabin website struggled with having a clear brand and mission for their visitors to see. It was difficult to find important information on the website due to a poor home page layout and an unclear navigation system. Our goal was to make the branding of the company clearer, simplify the navigation system to make it more user friendly, make the colors and images more inviting, and simplify the page layouts.

DEFINITION AND IDEATION

Problem Statement

One Small Step/Clothes Cabin's mission is to provide quality clothes and shoes to support individuals who are living in poverty. We have observed that the website isn’t offering enough transparency for donating users , which is causing a lack of trust and less donations through the website and to their business. How might we improve One Small Step so that the donations are successful based on recurring & new donations to the organization.

UX Hypothesis

We believe that providing a clear and well formatted website for recurring and potential donors will achieve our goal of increased donations and trust in our organization.

USER TESTING AND OUTCOMES

Testing

We tested our desktip low-fi prototype on 6 participants. These tests were completed in person and online by Ewa Berning and were reviewed later as a team. All participants were able to complete their testing without any difficulty. Feedback we recieved included not enjoying the way the dropdown menu slid into the screen and compliments on how easy it was to find everything on the website.

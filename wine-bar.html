Making Serving Easier

PROJECT OVERVIEW

OVERVIEW

I wanted to create a system that would help high end servers, specfically at wine bars and upscale restaurants, have an easier and more time efficient way to interact with their guests. A lot of time is wasted in the service industry because of slow and disorganized ordering systems, lack of knowledge by the staff on specific products, and lack of resources provided about the products they are selling. My goal was to create a system that will bring in more revenue to the restaurant and therefore, more money to the employees.

USER RESEARCH

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

Below is a heuristic evaluation of the ordering system, Micros, which is currently being used is over 75,000 restaurants in the US and is 1 of the 4 most used ordering systems in the world. A lot of issues were found while doing a heuristic evaluation of this ordering system, such as having unclear labeling, unclickable areas, and confusing user paths.

PROTO-PERSONA

I created a proto persona to show who the targeted user would be. It was important for me to make a user persona because of how very different people who work in the restuarant industry are, and yet they would all be impacted by an ordering system. This was especially important when taking into account any older users who might find navigating an ordering system more difficult.

DEFINITION AND IDEATION

PROBLEM STATEMENT

A restaurants mission is to server high quality products in a timely manner to their guests while giving them friendly and knowledgable service. It has been observed that the efficiency of servers is being hindered in a lot of restaurants because of the ordering systems they use, which is causing long wait times and dissatisfied customers. How might we improve upscale resaurant ordering systems so that guests are more satisfied and want to come back?

UX HYPOTHESIS

I believe that providing staff members with an easy to user ordering system that they can bring to tables will increase guests and employee satisfaction while increasing revenue for the company.

USER TESTING AND OUTCOMES

USABILITY TESTING

Success Rate: 100%

Task 2 Go to table 12.

Success Rate: 100%

Task 3. Go to red wines.

Success Rate: 80%

Task 4. Go to the pinot noir tab.

Success Rate: 100%

Task 5. Click on the one French wine in the pinot noir tab.

Success Rate: 80%

Task 6. Go back to the pinot noir tab.

Success Rate: 100%

3 tests were conducted on my low-fidelity prototype. Majority of tests were successful minus minor difficulities. Some vaulable feedback was received from participants:

-Be able to click on an item on the customers receipt and have it take you back to page where the item is located.

-Differentiate better between courses.

-Emphasize when a course have been completed.

-Add more indents.

-Add more back buttons.

-Show all wine options when just clicking red or white.

-Have grape vertical rather than have it an entire tab / be able to search the tab.

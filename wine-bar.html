Wine Bar Ordering System Design

A more efficient way for servers to put in orders, interact with guests, upsell, grow in their knowledge of company product, and inprove guest satisfaction.

I wanted to create a system that would help high end servers, specfically at wine bars and upscale restaurants, have an easier and more time efficient way to interact with their guests. A lot of time is wasted in the service industry because of slow and disorganized ordering systems, lack of knowledge by the staff on specific products, and lack of resources provided about the products they are selling. My goal was to create a system that will bring in more revenue to the restaurant and therefore, more money to the employees as well.

DEFINITION AND IDEATION

Problem Statement

A restaurants mission is to server high quality products in a timely manner to their guests while giving them friendly and knowledgable service. It has been observed that the efficiency of servers is being hindered in a lot of restaurants because of the ordering systems they use, which is causing long wait times and dissatisfied customers. How might we improve upscale resaurant ordering systems so that guests are more satified and want to come back?

UX Hypothesis

I believe that providing staff members with an easy to user ordering system that they can bring to tables with increase guests and employee satisfaction while improving revenue for the company.

3 tests were conducted on my low-fidelity prototype. Majority of tests were successful minus minor difficulities. Some vaulable feedback was recieved from participants:

-Be able to click on an item on the customers receipt and have it take you back to page where the item is located.

-Differentiate better between courses.

-Emphasize when a course have been completed.

-Add more indents.

-Add more back buttons.

-Show all wine options when just clicking red or white.

-Have grape vertical rather than have it an entire tab / be able to search the tab.

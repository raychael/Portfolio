A Feminist Fashion Blog

PROJECT OVERVIEW

OVERVIEW

Creation of the fashion blog PRK, a sustainable and inclusive fashion blog that educates its users on the harms of the fast fashion industry and assists them in finding more sustainable brands, resulting in users being passionate about the site, leading to higher website traffic.

MY ROLE

Lead UX/UI Designer

DELIVERABLES

High-Fidelity Mobile and Desktop Prototypes

TOOLS I USED

AdobeXD, Github, Visual Studio Code, Miro, Google Slides, Excel, Word

USER RESEARCH

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

To start off our research we conducted heuristic evaluations of three major competitors in the fashion blog industry consisting of two direct competitors, Vogue Magazine and Harper and Harley Blog, and one indirect competitor, Instagram. Our goal was to gain an understanding of how these sites operated and what they were doing well vs. what could be approved upon.

INTERVIEWS AND SURVEYS

Next it was vital to better understand our potential users and what is really missing for them when it comes to fashion resources. We utilized two research methods, one of which was more personalized one on one interviews that allowed us to dive deep into the questions we had. The second was sending out a survey, which was less personal but allowed us to reach a much wider audience.

45% said they are not very knowledgable of the impacts of fashion on the environment.

55% said they like seeing content about causes they care about, including climate change and body positivity.

36% said they dislike that a lot of fashion content is not environmentally friendly.

What asked what they would like to see on a fashion blog one user said "I would like to see brands that are conscience about fast-fashion."

IDEATION

PROBLEM STATEMENT

We believe that emphasizing accessibility and sustainability in a fashion blog for fashion enthusiasts of all ages and backgrounds will help generate awareness of the impact fashion has on individuality and the world.

USER PERSONA

It was important for us to acknowledge that not all the individuals we interviewed would necessarily be target users for our blog. We wanted to create a user persona that would represent our audience and appeal to individuals who would become regular readers. This approach allowed us to focus our efforts and create content that would resonate with our target audience, therefore improving engagement, returning users, and overall success of the website.

STORYBOARD

Going off of our user persona we put together a story board to show how our user persona, Mike, would feel about our blog and why it would be helpful to him.

LOW-FI PROTOTYPING

WIREFRAME SKETCHES

I then began wireframing by starting out with a few simple sketches of what I wanted the design to look like. My goal was to have simple but effective navigation and a modern and fun aesthetic for the site. The sketch below is for the home page.

LO-FI PROTOTYPING

After reviewing the sketches I moved into low-fi prototyping. I ended up changing the navigation to a hamburger menu as the original was making the site feeling overwheling and to give the site a more modern feel. I also wanted to have a bold logo in the center and did not want to take away from that. Majority of the other designs stayed the same.

USER TESTING AND OUTCOMES

USER TESTS

The user tests were extremely successful, with all three users being able to complete the assigned tasks:

1. Find an article to read. Success rate: 100%

2. Get to the inspiration page. Success rate: 100%

3. Find the about us page. Success rate: 100%

4. Go back to home page. Success rate: 100%

The feedback received from the users was largely positive, with many expressing that they enjoyed how simple and user-friendly the site was. However, I also received some feedback that users did not fully understand the purpose of the site due to the vague text. This feedback is crucial as I move into the high-fidelity prototyping stage.

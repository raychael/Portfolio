A Feminist Fashion Blog

Creation of the fashion blog PRK. A sustainable and inclusive fashion blog that educates its users on the harms of the fast fashion industry and helps them be more eco friendly.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

My Role

Co UX/UI Designer

Deliverables

High-Fidelity Mobile and Desktop Prototypes

Tools I Used

AdobeXD, Github, Visual Studio Code, Miro, Google Slides, Excel, Word

Timeline

02/10/22-02/17/22 User Research, Definition and Ideation

02/18/22-02/25/22 Prototyping, Testing, Iterations

02/26/22-03/01/22 Final Iterations Presenation Prep

My teammates and I came up with the idea of PRK through a brainstorming activity where we decided to make a fashion blog that strayed away from the traditional fashion related content found online. PRK is a fashion blog concerned with ethics and individual expression. We believe that if we can get people on board with sustainable fashion, we can reduce the carbon footprint of a notoriously unsustainalbe industry.

USER RESEARCH

Competitor Analysis

To kickstart our research, we initiated three competitor analyses that encompassed both direct and indirect competitors. This approach was deemed crucial given the fashion industry's saturation and our intention to establish a distinctive brand identity.

Direct Competitors: Harper and Harley Blog, Vogue Magazine.

Indirect Competitor: Instagram

Heuristic Evaluation

We then conducted a heuristic evaluation of these three competitors in order to focus more on the functionality of these popular sites.

User Research Plan and Interviews

Next it was vital to better understand our potential users and what is really missing for them when it comes to fashion resources. We utilized two research methods, one of which was more personalized one on one interviews that allowed us to dive deep into the questions we had. The second was sending out a survey, which was less personal but allowed us to reach a much wider audience.

Our Research Question: Why would users visit our fashion blog?

Objective 1: As a user researcher, I want to understand what users expectations are when visiting a fashion blog.

Objective 2: As a user research, I want to understand what users feelings are around fashion and what their concerns are.

Objective 3: As a user researcher, I want to understand how fashion impacts the lives of users and those around them.

IDEATION

Problem Statment

We believe that emphasizing accessibility and sustainability in a fashion blog for fashion enthusiasts of all ages and backgrounds will help generate awareness of the impact fashion has on individuality and the world.

User Persona

on the insights gathered from the interviews. However, it was important for us to acknowledge that not all the individuals we interviewed would necessarily be a good fit for our blog as we aimed to create a more targeted and niche user persona. We wanted to create a user persona that would represent our ideal audience and appeal to individuals who would become regular readers of our blog. This approach allowed us to focus our efforts and create content that would resonate with our target audience, thereby improving engagement and overall success of our blog.

User Scenario and Story Board

Going off of our user persona we put together a user scenario to show how our user persona, Mike, would feel about our blog and why it would be helpful to him.

Value Proposition

We then created a value proposition that users like Mike would be able to appreciate and relate to.

Value Proposition Statement

Our blog PRK is developing content and resources to help fashion enthusiasts bypass an oversaturation of fashion websites with excessive promotional links making it easier for users to find what they are searching for. We're better because we believe in promoting ethical practices and highly individualized style. We're believable because users rely on our content to navigate ethics in the fashion industry.

LOW-FI PROTOTYPING

Wireframe Sketches

I then began wireframing by starting out with a few simple sketches of what I wanted to design to look like. My goal was to have simple but effective navigation and a modern and clean aesthetic for the site. The sketch below is for the home page.

Low-Fi Prototype

After reviewing the sketches I moved into low-fi prototyping. I ended up changing the navigation to a hamburger menu as the original was making the site feeling overwhelimg. I also wanted to have a bold logo in the center and did not want to take away from that. Majority of the other designs stayed the same.

USER TESTING AND OUTCOMES

User Tests

The user tests were extremely successful, with all three users being able to complete the assigned tasks:

1. Find an article to read. Success rate: 100%

2. Get to the inspiration page. Success rate: 100%

3. Find the about us page. Success rate: 100%

4. Go back to home page. Success rate: 100%

The feedback received from the users was largely positive, with many expressing that they enjoyed how simple and user-friendly the site was. However, I also received some feedback that users did not fully understand the purpose of the site due to the vague text. This feedback is crucial as I move into the high-fidelity prototyping stage.

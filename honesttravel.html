Travel App Onboarding

Creation of the onboarding process of the app HonestTravel. A travel app that focuses on setting users up with their dream travel destination using the user's personality.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

My Role

Lead UX/UI Designer

Deliverables

High-Fidelity Mobile

Tools I Used

AdobeXD, Figma, Miro, Google Slides, Excel, Word, Google Drive

Timeline

09/09/21-09/14/21 User Research

09/15/21-09/21/21 Definition and Ideation

09/22/21-09/28/21 Paper Prototyping and Testing

09/29/21-10/02/21 iOS Propotyping and Testing

I came up with the idea for HonestTravel after several interviews and research on the current travel app market. For this project I focused on the onboarding process of HonestTravel, getting all necessary information from users while describing the apps features so the user can know what to expect from using the app.

USER RESEARCH

Proto Persona

To begin research for what type of travel app to build I created a user-persona of what I believed a potential user would look like.

Interviews

I conducted 5 face-to-face interviews with individuals ages 21-60 to discover how satisfied people usually are with their choice of trip destinations. I discovered that it was very common to be surprised by the travel location once arriving, in both good and bad ways.

Affinity Diagram

To created the affinity diargram I started by conducting an I Like, I Wish, What If excercise keeping in mind the information obtained from the interviews. From there my partner and I arranged our ideas into categories.

Empathy Map

We also created an empathy map and a user persona both based off the results from our interviews.

User Persona

IDEATION

User Insight Statement

A young traveler needs to have a better insight into what a place is really like before deciding to visit so that they can be prepared for what their trip is going to be like and accurately set their expectations and not end up disappointed.

Problem Statment

Our app was designed to achieve helping travelers understand what a place is really like before going. We have observed that our product isn’t meeting our goal of travelers to feel more ready for their travels, which is causing a decrease in usage to our app. How might we improve our app so that our customers are successful based on an increase in app usage, a better understanding of their travel destination, and an increase in happiness after their travels?

Feature Prioritization Matrix

From the ideas we came up with from our affinity diagram we created a feature prioritization matrix to see what features we could realistically impliment into our app.

Value Proposition Statement

My organization HonestTravel is developing a way to pick the right travel destination and set more accurate expectations when going to a new location to help travelers solve the risk of being disappointed by a vacation. We’re better because we don’t try to sell you on a specific destination, rather we match you up with destinations based on your personality and interests and help set your expectations accordingly. We’re believable because we rely on reviews from people who have already been to the destination and tips from locals.

User Scenario

A user scenario was made to show why users would us our app.

Storyboard

And then a storyboard was put togehter to further illustrate our potential users.

User Journey Map

The last step in our ideation phase was to make a user journey map to detect any potential problems our users might experience.

LOW-FI PROTOTYPING

Competitor Analysis

Before we began our prototyping we conducted competitor analysis' of three competitors. We looked at their strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and potential threats.

User Flow

Next we made a user flow diagram to show all the potential paths the user could take while signing up or logging in.

Wireframe Sketches

I then began wireframing by starting out with a few simple sketches, prototyping these sketches, and conducting user tests to ensure the flow was natural and easy for users to navigate through.

Low-Fi Prototype

After receiving positing results from the paper prototypes I moved into the low-fidelity wireframing and prototyping phase and got ready for additional testing.

USER TESTING AND OUTCOMES

Guerrilla User Testing Plan

5 tests were conducted on the low-fidelity prototypes. Task 1 and 2 recieved a 90% success rate and task 3 recieved a 100% success rate amount participants. I received a lot of valuable feedback from my Guerrilla user tests and from those tests I was able to implement several iterations into my prototype. Such as adding a confirmation message after signing up.

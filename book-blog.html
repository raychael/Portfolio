Convincing You To Read

PROJECT OVERVIEW

OVERVIEW

A personal book blog that includes ratings and reviews of all the books I've read recently and lists of the ones I want to read, with the goal of helping users decide on the perfect book for them to read next.

MY ROLE

Lead UX/UI Designer

DELIVERABLES

High-Fidelity Mobile and Desktop Prototypes

TOOLS I USED

AdobeXD, Github, Visual Studio Code, Miro, Google Slides, Excel, Word

I came up with the idea for creating my own personalized book blog because of my own love for books and experiencing a lack of options for online book review websites. I noticed fellow book club members and friends experiencing the same issues as I was and wanted to provide a blog that makes finding reviews of popular books easy and fun.

USER RESEARCH

INTERVIEWS

The goal of this website is to provide a resource for book lovers to find new books to read and send recommendations to their friends. Because this website is made for a very specific audience I wanted to start out by conducting 5 user interviews that would help me gain a deeper insight into who these users are and what they desire from a book review website.

Research Question: What would users want out of a book review website?

80% are unhappy with the current market of book review sites.

80% said they don't have any sites they trust the reviews of enough to use regularly.

100% said they would be excited to visit a website from a reviewer they trust.

PROTO-PERSONA

The next step in my research was to come up with a user I believe would want to visit and stay on the website. Since I was making this site for a very specific audience of book enthusiasts it was necessary to have a clear picture of those users. The proto persona was heavily inspired by my five interview subjects.

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

I then conducted a heuristic evaluation of three popular book review websites. Goodreads, Library Thing, and Reedsy Discovery. These websites were frequently mentioned in the user interviews, specifcally Goodreads, so conducting heuristic evaluations was important in understanding why many users were unhappy with these sites.

IDEATION

USER SCENARIO

I then put together a user scenario to show how the user would feel about and interact with the blog, to further demonstrate the impact of a good book reviewing website on its users.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

We believe creating a user friendly book blog with detailed trusted reviews will lead our users to gain a trust for the website, recommend the site to their friends, and visit again in the future.

LOW-FI WIREFRAMES

LO-FI PROTOTYPE

The first step to putting all the research together was creating low-fit wireframes. The goal here was easy navigation, clear titls, and to not be too overwhelming with the amount of information available.

USER TESTING AND OUTCOMES

USER TESTS

Most user tests went well with miminal issues navigating the website. However, I learned from user testing and feedback that some additional iterations needed to be made:

1. Add star ratings for all the books on the main reviews page.

2. Be able to filter by 1-5 star ratings.

3. Get rid of "books I recommend for new readers" because they will be similar to my 5 star ratings.

4. Add social media icons.

STYLE TILE AND HIGH-FI PROTOTYPING

STYLE TILE

I then moved into creating a high-fidelity prototype starting out with a style tile. I wanted to incorporate a lot of interesting typography since the theme of the website is books and have a more simple color scheme that would work with the many different book covers that will be on the site.
